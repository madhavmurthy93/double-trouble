Doing another major or minor with Bioengineering

We have created this tool to assist Bioengineering undergraduate students at UW who are interested in pursuing another major or minor in addition to Bioengineering. This tool details the coursework, workload, and background required to pursue another degree. We also have provided the contact information to reach out to Bioengineering students from previous years who have pursued additional majors or minors. These are the list of majors/minors that we think are most relevant to Bioengineering students: Biochemistry Bioethics & Humanities Biology Chemistry Chemical Engineering Computer Science & Engineering Electrical Engineering Global Health Human Centered Design & Engineering Information School Materials Science and Engineering Mathematics Mechanical Engineering Microbiology Neurobiology

Biochemistry

The skills learned in the biochemistry department are likely very similar to those offered within the bioengineering program. The primary benefit of adding Biochemistry as a second major will be to show additional expertise and perhaps demonstrate specific bench-top skills. Website: https://depts.washington.edu/biowww/pages/undergraduate.shtml

Bioethics & Humanities

Bioethics & Humanities could be a useful minor for bioengineering students interested in complementing their technical background with softer skills in ethics and philosophy. The program is designed for students planning on working in the health sciences. The minor claims that it is best to plan for taking two years to complete due to sporadic course offerings. Website: https://depts.washington.edu/bhdept/index.html

Interesting Classes

Examines the issues of medical ethics from a patient's point of view. [/toggle] [toggle title="B H 460 Responsible Conduct of Research (3, I&S)"] Explores ethical and policy issues that energy in the conduct of basic, applied, translational, community-based, and collaborative research. Chemistry

The Bioengineering background in chemistry goes through organic chemistry and an introduction to biomolecules (BIOEN 315). To develop a further specialization in chemistry, or to develop additional skills for the purpose of career development or research, a second major or a minor in Chemistry might be relevant. Information School

Bioengineering students interested either in data science, analytics, or security can also pursue a major in Informatics. Informatics places an emphasis on the derivation of actionable knowledge from datasets - which can align well with research and other postgraduate goals. Informatics also has specializations in security and human-computer interactions. Website: https://ischool.uw.edu/

Mathematics

The fundamental engineering concepts used in Bioengineering are all based off of Mathematics. The prerequisite courses extend through Math 307 & 308, concluding with the ability to solve differential equations and perform matrix math. Pursuing a major or minor in Math will allow for further explanation of the theorems and concepts that are the foundation of bioengineering teachings. Website: http://www.math.washington.edu/
